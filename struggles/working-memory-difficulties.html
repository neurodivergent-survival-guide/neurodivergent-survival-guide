What This Feels Like

Working memory difficultiesChallenges with temporarily holding and manipulating information in your mind while working on tasks often feel like having a mental workspace that's too small for the job you're trying to do. Many people describe it as feeling like they can only hold one or two pieces of information in their head at once before everything else falls out.

You might walk into a room to get something and immediately forget what you came for. During conversations, you lose track of what you were going to say while listening to the other person. Multi-step instructionsDirections involving several sequential actions that can overwhelm working memory capacity feel overwhelming - you understand the first step but struggle to hold the remaining steps in mind while executing.

This creates a constant feeling of mental juggling where you're afraid to think about anything else because you might drop the information you're currently holding. Cognitive loadThe amount of mental effort being used in your working memory during thinking tasks feels heavy even during simple tasks, leaving you mentally exhausted from activities that seem effortless for others.

Common experiences: Forgetting instructions immediately after hearing them; losing your train of thought mid-sentence; needing to write everything down; feeling overwhelmed by tasks with multiple steps; forgetting what you're doing while doing it.
