What Are Neurodivergent Conditions?

Some people's brains work differently from what society considers "typical." These differences aren't diseases or problems to fixâ€”they're just variations in how human brains can develop and function.

These brain differences can create both challenges and strengths. The challenges usually come from living in a world designed for brains that work differently than yours. Understanding your brain type can help you make sense of daily struggles and find better ways to navigate the world.

Each condition affects people differently. Two people with autism, for example, might have completely different daily experiences. Think of these as starting points for understanding yourself, not boxes that define you.

How to Use This Section

  • If you suspect a specific condition: Click on that condition to learn more about how it shows up in daily life
  • If you're exploring multiple possibilities: Read through several conditions to see what resonates
  • If you're newly diagnosed: Start with your condition to understand how it might affect different areas of your life
  • If you're supporting someone else: Learn about their condition to better understand their experiences

Important: This Is Not for Diagnosis

This information is designed to help you understand yourself better and communicate with healthcare providers. It cannot diagnose conditionsâ€”only qualified professionals can do that.

Many experiences overlap between conditions, and many people have multiple conditions at once. Use this information as a starting point for conversations with doctors, therapists, or other professionals.
